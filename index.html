Brief History of Cordova


- CORDOVA is NOT the original name of territory it represents, NOT even the SPANISH name CORDOBA. It's territory comprises the original barrios ("balangays" in Cebuano) of Gabi, Day-as and Pilipul (actually Pilipog) - all of which are located in the southeastern trip of Mactan Island.

CORDOBA means "stark nakedness and bare".

On December 1, 1912
Governor-General Newtown W. Gilbert issued an ORDEN EJECUTIVA NUMBERO 96 (EXECUTIVE ORDER NUMBER 96) recreating the new town but changing letter "B" to "V" to spell CORDOVA effective January, 1913.

Environment


- Mangrove conservation in Cordova is more than a contemporary environmental practice; it is an investment in the town's heritage and the well-being of future generations.




- The allure of Cordova's coastal beauty draws visitors from far and wide, but with it comes the challenge of managing waste responsibly. Cordova's local government, along with passionate community groups, has embraced waste management initiatives.



Cordova recognizes the importance of community engagement in sustaining its environmental initiatives. Tree-planting activities, consciousness among residents and visitors alike, fostering a culture of respect for nature.
